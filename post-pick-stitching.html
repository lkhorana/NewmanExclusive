Design Details

Pick or No Pick Stitching?

Some of you may have noticed a visible stitch on the exterior of certain jackets. This visible stitch, often found on high-quality outerwear, serves both a functional and aesthetic purpose. From a practical standpoint, this stitch reinforces the seams of the jacket, ensuring durability and longevity.

It adds an extra layer of strength to the garment, especially in areas prone to wear and tear. Moreover, this visible stitch has become a design element in its own right. Fashion designers often incorporate exposed stitching as a stylistic choice to add a touch of uniqueness and character to the jacket.

Function Meets Artistry

The presence of this visible stitch highlights the craftsmanship that goes into creating the jacket. It showcases the attention to detail and precision involved in its construction. This subtle yet significant feature underscores the quality and authenticity of the garment, distinguishing it from mass-produced alternatives.

Common Inquiries at Newman Exclusive:

• Why is the stitch on the outside?
• Is the jacket completed?
• Is the stitch just a cosmetical feature or does it actually have any purpose?

Heritage and Character

In the olden days, pick stitching did serve the purpose of making sure that all the inner layers of the suit jacket were being held together properly. However, nowadays, it's more about showing off more customization to your custom-made, tailored suit.

Keep in mind that any hand-done work on your tailored suit jacket and/or pants adds to its production time and cost, giving it more character. Exploring these aspects can help you appreciate the craftsmanship and attention to detail that goes into creating a truly unique and personalized garment.
